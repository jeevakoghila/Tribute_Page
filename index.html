Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Missile Man of India

Here's a timeline of Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam's life:

  • 1931: Born on October 15, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India.
  • 1954: Graduated in Physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli.
  • 1955: Joined the Aeronautical Development Establishment of Defense Research and Development Organisation (DRDO).
  • 1960s: Played a crucial role in India's space program, particularly in the development of satellite launch vehicles.
  • 1980s: Directed the Integrated Guided Missile Development Program (IGMDP).
  • 1992-1997: Served as the Scientific Advisor to the Defense Minister.
  • 1998-2001: Appointed as the Principal Scientific Advisor to the Government of India.
  • 2002-2007: Served as the 11th President of India.
  • Post-Presidency: Continued to be an advocate for education, science, and youth development until his passing.
